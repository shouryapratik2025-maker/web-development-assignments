5 Cybersecurity Habits Everyone Should Practice

👤 Alex Kumar 📅 Apr 5, 2025 🕑 5 min read

You don't need to be a security expert to protect yourself online. Most successful cyberattacks succeed because of simple, preventable mistakes. Here are five habits that make a meaningful difference.

1. Use a Password Manager

Reusing passwords is the single biggest mistake most people make. If one site gets hacked, attackers try your password on every other site — this is called credential stuffing. A password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password, etc.) generates and stores unique, complex passwords for every site so you only remember one master password.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds a second layer: even if someone steals your password, they can't log in without the second factor (usually a code from your phone). Enable it on your email, bank, and any account that offers it. App-based authenticators (Google Authenticator, Authy) are more secure than SMS codes.

"Enabling 2FA on your email account is the single most impactful security action most people can take."

3. Keep Software Updated

Most major breaches exploit known vulnerabilities in outdated software. When your OS or browser prompts an update, that update almost certainly contains security patches. Enable automatic updates for your operating system, browser, and any critical apps.

4. Be Sceptical of Links and Attachments

Phishing — tricking you into clicking a malicious link — is behind a huge proportion of security incidents. Before clicking any link in an email or message: hover over it to see the real URL, check for subtle misspellings in the domain name, and ask yourself whether you were expecting this message.

5. Back Up Your Data Regularly

Ransomware attacks encrypt your files and demand payment for the key. Your best defence is a backup that attackers can't reach. Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of your data, on 2 different media types, with 1 stored offsite (or in the cloud).
