Understanding Cloud Computing in Plain English

👤 Alex Kumar 📅 Apr 14, 2025 🕑 5 min read

"It's in the cloud" — you've heard it dozens of times. But what does it actually mean? Cloud computing sounds abstract, but the concept is surprisingly straightforward once you strip away the marketing language.

The Simple Definition

Cloud computing means using computers, storage, and software owned by someone else, accessed over the internet, instead of running everything locally on your own hardware. Instead of buying a powerful server and putting it in your office, you rent time on Amazon's, Google's, or Microsoft's massive data centres.

Three Service Models

Cloud services come in three main forms:

  • IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service): Raw computing power, storage, and networking. Example: Amazon EC2.
  • PaaS (Platform as a Service): A ready-to-use development environment. Example: Google App Engine.
  • SaaS (Software as a Service): Full applications delivered over the web. Example: Gmail, Dropbox, Notion.

Why Businesses Love the Cloud

The cloud offers massive advantages: you only pay for what you use (no wasted hardware), you can scale up in minutes instead of months, your data is backed up automatically, and your team can access systems from anywhere in the world.

"The cloud didn't change what companies do — it changed how fast and cheaply they can do it."

Common Misconceptions

"The cloud is someone else's computer" is a meme, but it's also mostly true. The key insight is that the abstraction layer above that computer — the elasticity, the managed services, the global distribution — is what actually makes cloud computing transformative, not just the fact that servers exist somewhere.
