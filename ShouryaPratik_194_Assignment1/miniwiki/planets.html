Inner Planets — The Terrestrial Four

The four inner planets — Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars — are called terrestrial planets because they have solid, rocky surfaces, similar to Earth (Latin: terra, meaning land).

Key Characteristics

Composition

All four are composed primarily of silicate rock and metals, with relatively thin atmospheres compared to the outer giants.

Fun Sub-fact

Earth and Venus are often called "twin planets" because they are similar in size and mass — yet Venus is uninhabitable while Earth thrives with life.

Outer Planets — Gas & Ice Giants

Beyond the asteroid belt lie the four giant planets. Jupiter and Saturn are gas giants — enormous balls of hydrogen and helium. Uranus and Neptune are ice giants with large amounts of water, ammonia, and methane ices.

Did you know? Jupiter is so massive that it has more mass than all other planets combined, multiplied by twice.
